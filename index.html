Walter Elias Disney, commonly known as Walt Disney, was a visionary American animator, film producer, and entrepreneur. Born on December 5, 1901, in Chicago, Illinois, he co-founded the Walt Disney Company and is celebrated for creating iconic characters like Mickey Mouse and for pioneering animated films. Disney was a key figure in the development of animation and family entertainment, known for his creativity and innovation. His work led to the creation of Disneyland and Walt Disney World, setting a standard for theme parks worldwide. Walt Disney passed away on December 15, 1966, leaving a lasting legacy in the world of entertainment.
